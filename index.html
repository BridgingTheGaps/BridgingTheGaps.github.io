Explaining BTG a bit more

Why ?

In India, we live in a tremendously unequal society. Our mission is to inspire a generation of Indian youth to bridge the socio-economic gaps created by poverty, gender and class, through a uniquely designed sport-art camp. Our camps build and foster important values such as gender equality and open-mindedness, that translate into impactful benefits to the young campers, both players and coaches.

Where?

This December's camp will be held in Auroville, a small city located south of Chennai, about 2.5 hrs by bus/car and home to a vibrant international community including many talented and spirited Ultimate Frisbee players.

We’ve done this before...

and we'll do it again! Our first camp was held in Surat in May 2014 where 120 teen campers and 30+ coaches had a 5 day experience of a lifetime! This December we'll bring 150 kids and 35+ coaches to Auroville and plans are already in the works for our next camp in May 2015.

How participants, youth leaders and coaches benefit

Many of our coaches and youth leaders...

are from the same communities as our participants. They face a similar uphill climb with regards to poverty, gender and class based discrimination.

Role Model Training

Our coaches and youth leaders attend ‘role model training’ sessions before camp begins. This prepares our coaches and youth leaders to facilitate meaningful discussions, teach the technical Ultimate Frisbee curriculum and conduct team building exercises.

Why Ultimate Frisbee ?

For this camp, we have chosen to use Ultimate Frisbee because of how well it lends itself to promote personal development and intercultural exchange.

Ultimate is unique and best suited for Bridging the Gaps because it is:

  • Co-ed & non-contact: Boys and girls play together on a team - an aberration to cultural practices within Indian communities where boys and girls rarely mix in sport.
  • Self refereed: Contested fouls are settled through discussion between the players involved. This facilitates open communication between players and teaches participants how to resolve conflict. It is rare for a sport to allow for this type of player-to-player dialogue.
  • Spirit of the Game (SOTG): SOTG is a heightened version of sportsmanship where competitive play is encouraged, but never at the expense of respect between players, adherence to the rules, and the basic joy of play. SOTG is built into the rules of the sport.
